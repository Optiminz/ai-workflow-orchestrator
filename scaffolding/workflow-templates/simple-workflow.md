**Minimum personas. Fastest cycle. For low-stakes or highly familiar projects.**

- [PLACEHOLDER — e.g., Routine internal documents where quality threshold is lower than client-facing work]

**Skipped personas:**
- [PLACEHOLDER — e.g., 02 — Researcher] — [PLACEHOLDER — e.g., No new research needed]
- [PLACEHOLDER — e.g., 03 — Strategist] — [PLACEHOLDER — e.g., Strategy is clear — brief provided directly to Creator]

> Simple produces the weakest output of the three variants. Default to Quick or Full when in doubt. If a Simple output scores below 70, the project warranted a higher-complexity workflow.

## Important: Simple Limitations

**Simple removes the safety net that Researcher and Strategist provide.** This means:

1. **The Qualifier must do more work.** When Strategist is skipped, the Qualifier brief must include the strategic direction. If the Qualifier brief is vague, the Creator has no guidance and will produce generic output.

2. **No research means no specific signals.** Simple output will be less personalised by design. Accept this trade-off only when personalisation is not a key success factor.

3. **RESTRUCTURE verdict means Full, not revision.** If Simple produces a RESTRUCTURE verdict, do not iterate on Simple. Upgrade to Full. The structural problem is almost certainly caused by the missing Researcher + Strategist, not fixable with iteration alone.

---

## Upgrade Paths

**Upgrade to Quick when:**
- Output is consistently feeling generic despite good Qualifier briefs
- The subject type benefits from specific signal identification
- Critic scores below 75 on Personalisation

**Upgrade to Full when:**
- RESTRUCTURE or RELEASE verdict from Simple
- High-stakes project got accidentally assigned Simple
- You need to establish a quality baseline for a new project type

**How to upgrade mid-project:**
1. Save the Qualifier output (it's still valid)
2. Start fresh at Researcher (Quick) or at Researcher + Strategist (Full)
3. Note the upgrade in MEMORY.md: "Upgraded Simple → [Full/Quick]. Reason: [specific reason]."
